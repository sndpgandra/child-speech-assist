# Speech Practice Application - Business Use case

## Use case Overview

The Speech Practice Application is a digital therapy tool designed to help children with childhood apraxia of speech delay to practice their communication skills at home. This application bridges the gap between clinical speech therapy sessions and home practice, providing parents with an easy-to-use platform to support their child's speech development journey.

## Business Problem

Children with apraxia or speech delay require intensive, repetitive practice to improve their communication abilities. However:

- **Limited therapy access**: Families often face long wait times and limited session availability with speech therapists
- **High costs**: Professional speech therapy sessions can be expensive and may not be fully covered by insurance
- **Practice gap**: Children need daily practice between therapy sessions, but parents lack structured tools to facilitate this
- **Engagement challenges**: Traditional practice methods can be boring for children, leading to resistance and reduced practice time
- **Progress tracking**: Parents struggle to monitor their child's improvement and identify which words need more focus

## Solution Overview

A mobile-friendly web first application that enables children to practice speech independently while giving parents complete control over content and progress tracking. 

## Key User Groups

### Primary Users: Children (Ages 3-10)
Children use a simplified, colorful interface to practice words through visual flashcards or small video clips with pronunciation demonstrations.

### Secondary Users: Parents/Caregivers
Parents manage the application through a secure admin area where they can customize content, upload materials, and track their child's progress.

## Core Capabilities

### secondary data source
- will use the generative AI and video creation tools to create the small clips of videos for the words. ex: lip moment of the word pop / cat / apple etc.

### For Children - Practice Mode

**Visual Learning Experience**
- identify the type of content to practice (e.g., flashcards, videos)
- select the category of words to practice (e.g., animals, fruits, vegetables)
- select the words to practice (e.g., cat, apple, carrot)
- Children see large, clear images or videos paired with words to practice
- Simple navigation allows them to move through practice words at their own pace
- Pronunciation demonstrations show exactly how to form sounds and words
- Colorful, engaging design keeps children motivated and focused

**Independent Practice**
- Children can practice on their own without constant parent supervision
- Large buttons and simple controls are designed for small hands and developing motor skills
- No reading required - visual cues guide the entire experience

### For Parents - Management & Tracking

**Content Customization**
- Organize practice words into meaningful categories (e.g., "Foods," "Animals," "Family")
- Upload custom images and pronunciation videos specific to your child's therapy goals
- Add, edit, or remove words as your child progresses through their therapy plan
- Arrange content in the order that works best for your child's learning

**Progress Monitoring**
- Track which words your child is learning, practicing, or has mastered
- Update word status to reflect your child's current skill level
- View overall progress across all practice categories
- Identify areas that need more focus or celebration

## User Journey Examples

### Typical Child Practice Session

1. Child opens the app and sees colorful category cards (e.g., "Animals," "Foods")
2. Child taps on "Animals" category
3. App shows first word "Dog" with a large picture of a dog
4. Child attempts to say the word
5. Child taps "Hear Pronunciation" button to see video demonstration
6. Child watches mouth movements and tries again
7. Child taps "Next" to move to the next word
8. Progress bar shows they've completed 5 of 10 words
9. Child continues practicing at their own pace

### Typical Parent Management Session

1. Parent accesses admin area with password
2. Dashboard shows: 3 categories, 25 total words, 8 mastered
3. Parent clicks "Manage Words" for "Foods" category
4. Parent sees word "Apple" is marked as "Practicing"
5. Child has been saying "Apple" correctly all week
6. Parent updates status to "Mastered"
7. Parent adds new word "Banana" with photo and pronunciation video
8. Changes are saved and immediately available for child's next practice session
